## Background

I'm a security researcher working on problems at the intersection of post-quantum cryptography, AI agent security, and covert channel analysis.

Before this, I spent years in the U.S. Marine Corps (Reconnaissance), where I learned that the most dangerous threats are the ones you don't see coming. That principle drives my research: finding the attack surfaces that haven't been mapped yet.

## Current Research

**LLM Covert Channels** — Large language models can carry hidden information in their outputs through statistical manipulation of token distributions, structural encoding, and timing patterns. I'm building detection tools and publishing findings on this emerging threat class.

**Post-Quantum Cryptography** — NIST has finalized FIPS 203/204/205. The migration window is closing. I work on practical PQC implementation and Harvest-Now-Decrypt-Later (HNDL) detection.

**AI Agent Authentication** — As AI agents proliferate in enterprise environments, authenticating their identity and detecting compromised agents becomes critical. I research behavioral entropy signatures as a fingerprinting mechanism.
